tiny trainable instruments

abstract

tiny trainable instruments was my master's thesis at MIT Media Lab, where i was a research assistant at the Opera of the Future and Future Sketches research groups between 2019-2021.

this thesis was an exploration of artistic and musical expression using the nascent field of tiny machine learning, for low-fidelity use of artificial intelligence using microcontrollers.

software library

the software library for this project is available at the repository https://github.com/montoyamoraga/TinyTrainable, and it was also published at the Arduino library registry and can be installed directly from the IDE.

workshops

this thesis project included teaching 3 workshops (2 in English for USA, 1 in Spanish for Chile), and won an MIT grant for funding the materials and shipping costs to all participants.

10 people joined each instance, for a total of 30 students, and 12 hours teaching to beginners with no programming or machine learning experience.
